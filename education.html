Getting Started with Linux in Education

1 Start Small

Begin with a single classroom or computer lab. Test with teachers who are tech-savvy. Use live USBs to demonstrate without installation.

2 Train Teachers

Provide professional development. Linux is different but not difficult. Focus on educational benefits, not technical details.

3 Deploy Gradually

Use imaging tools to deploy identical configurations. Start with computer labs, then administrative offices, then teacher workstations.

4 Join Community

Connect with other schools using Linux. Share configurations, lesson plans, and get support from educational Linux communities.
